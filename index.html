I am a fourth-year PhD student working in the Department of Mathematics at the University of Iowa, advised by Prof. Mohammad Tehrani. My research lies at the intersection of algebraic geometry and number theory, with a particular focus on arithmetic aspects of moduli spaces.

Before coming to Somewhere, I completed my undergraduate degree at Another University, where I wrote a thesis on étale cohomology under the supervision of Prof. Mary Jones.

Research

My primary interests lie in arithmetic algebraic geometry and number theory. More specifically, I am interested in:

Algebraic Geometry Arithmetic Geometry Moduli Spaces Étale Cohomology Shimura Varieties p-adic Methods Langlands Program

Papers & Preprints

  • On the Geometry of Certain Shimura Varieties of Hodge Type
    Preprint, 2025 · arXiv:2501.XXXXX
    We study the integral models of Shimura varieties associated to certain symplectic groups and establish flatness results for their special fibres using deformation-theoretic techniques.
  • Étale Cohomology of Moduli Spaces of Abelian Varieties (with A. Coauthor)
    Submitted, 2024 · arXiv:2412.XXXXX
    We compute the étale cohomology groups of certain moduli stacks of principally polarised abelian varieties with level structure, and derive applications to the Langlands correspondence.
  • A Note on p-adic Interpolation of L-functions
